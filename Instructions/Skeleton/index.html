Smoke on the Water

Smoking is bad for you, being associated with an increased risk of premature death and chronic health conditions. Given this, I considered factors that might be associated with increased smoking in different states. I did a bit of background research and I noted was that people in certain industries are more likely to smoke. With this in mind I investigated if some states were outliers using the industry of production, transportation and material moving as a model since the CDC article mentions production (manufacturing) and transportation specifically. I've also talked with people personally in manufacturing jobs who say they took up smoking as a way to get more breaks, unfortunately.

West Virginia is an outlier in this dataset because only 12% of the state is employed in production, transportation, and material moving, but 23% of West Virginians smoke. This isn't that surprising as West Virginia is one of the nation's worst states for tobacco use This may be due to West Virginia being a state with more poverty and less education than is typical in the rest of the US, as well as low cigarette taxes.
Utah is an outlier in the opposite direction. The rate of employement in these industries is similar to West Virginia, but a mere 6% of people smoke in the state. This has a large part to do with religion, a majority of people in the state identify as member of the Church of Later Day Saints(Mormons). Members of the church do not typically use drugs or alcohol and tobacco use is culturally frowned upon.
